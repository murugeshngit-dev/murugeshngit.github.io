Murugesh Narayanappa, Ph.D. (Biochemistry and Biophysics)

San Jose, California | murugeshnpadma@gmail.com | GitHub

Professional Summary

Senior scientist with multidisciplinary experience in protein biochemistry, biophysics, and functional genomics, and is complemented by 4+ years of computational biology and advanced quantitative and statistical skills. I leverage both experimental and computational approaches to address diverse biological questions, from mechanistic understanding to innovative therapeutic strategies for drug discovery. Experienced in mentoring and leading collaborative teams, I seek to contribute to innovative discoveries as a Senior or Principal Scientist.

Skills

  • Protein Biochemistry & Biophysics: Protein expression (mammalian, bacterial, insect cells) and purification (affinity, ion-exchange, SEC) using FPLC; Biophysical characterization: ITC, CD, fluorescence spectroscopy, BLI (OctetRed); Protein engineering, computational modeling, structural analysis (ChimeraX, AlphaFold3).
  • Molecular & Cell Biology: PCR/qPCR, molecular cloning (restriction, Gibson, Golden Gate assembly); Mammalian cell culture (eHAP1, RKO, HEK293T), transient transfection, stable cell line generation; Western blotting, immunofluorescence/confocal microscopy, flow cytometry (FlowJo).
  • Functional Genomics & Computational Biology: Library design and preparation for CRISPR knockout and base editing screens; CRISPR/base editing screens, RNA-seq analysis using custom R/Bash scripts, DESeq2, MaGeCK; NGS sample preparation and analysis, including UMI-based read processing; Hands-on experience with MiSeq and NextSeq sequencing platforms.
  • Programming & Data Visualization: Proficient in R, Bash for genomic data analysis and visualization on HPC; Statistical analysis: regression modeling, ANOVA, multivariate analysis, inference; Familiar with Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn) for data manipulation and plotting.

Professional Experience

Stanford University – Senior Scientist (Life Science Research Professional), 2022–Present

Advisor: Dr. Rajat Rohatgi, MD, PhD

  • Developed and optimized CRISPR knockout and base editing screens, including library design, preparation, and NGS workflows (MiSeq, NextSeq) to map sequence-function landscapes within β-catenin destruction complex (CTNNB1, AXIN1, APC, GSK3β).
  • Integrated structural analysis (ChimeraX) and biophysical characterization (BLI) to define regulatory interfaces and mechanistic insights in WNT signaling.
