About Me

I am Murugesh Narayanappa, a biochemist and biophysicist with experience spanning protein biochemistry and biophysics, genetics and genome editing, and computational analysis. I completed my undergraduate degree in Pharmacy, driven by an early interest in human health, disease mechanisms, and drug action, and earned a master’s degree in Drug Design in the UK, where I worked at Agilent Technologies on high-throughput assays and developed a passion for protein biochemistry.

I earned a Ph.D. in Biochemistry and Biophysics at Oregon State University, focusing on the biophysical characterization of proteins involved in hearing loss and muscular dystrophy. Seeking stronger physiological context, I joined Dr. Jeremy Dittman’s lab at Weill Cornell Medicine, where I combined biochemical, biophysical, and genetic approaches to study synaptic proteins, expanded into genome editing, and applied cell-based and functional assays in model systems.

At Stanford University, I transitioned to signaling biology, studying the WNT destruction complex and its roles in development and cancer. I established CRISPR knockout and base-editing screens, developed scalable computational pipelines for high-throughput data analysis, and integrated protein biochemistry, molecular biology, and Next-generation sequencing (NGS) approaches to dissect complex biological mechanisms. My work now spans genome editing, transcriptomics, protein–protein and protein–lipid interactions, with a focus on mechanistic insight. My goal to combine experimental and computational methods to address challenging problems in biology.
